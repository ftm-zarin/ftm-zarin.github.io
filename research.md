<h1>Research</h1>

<p class="research-intro"> My research interests lie in applied cryptography, specifically proof systems. I am particularly interested in designing efficient and verifiable cryptographic protocols. </p>

<div class="research-list">

<article class="research-card">
  <div class="research-meta">2024 – 2026</div>
  <h2>ZKRNN: Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Recurrent Neural Networks</h2>
  <p class="research-place">
    The University of Sheffield & Sharif University of Technology
  </p>
  <p>
    I worked on cryptographic protocols for generating succinct
    zero-knowledge proofs for recurrent neural network inference. The goal
    was to make sequential machine-learning computations verifiable while
    keeping the verifier efficient.
  </p>
  <p class="research-links">
    <a href="https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/073.pdf" target="_blank">ePrint</a>
  </p>
  <p class="research-advisors">
    Advisors: Dr. Behzad Abdolmaleki and Dr. Shahram Khazaei
  </p>
</article>

<article class="research-card">
  <div class="research-meta">Summer 2025</div>
  <h2>Secure E-Voting with Everlasting Privacy</h2>
  <p class="research-place">
    École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)
  </p>
  <p>
    I studied trust-limited, coercion-resistant in-person voter registration
    for secure electronic voting. The project focused on protecting voters
    against forced voting while aiming for post-quantum everlasting privacy.
  </p>
  <p>
    A central idea was to rely on information-theoretic guarantees where
    possible, so that privacy can remain meaningful even against future
    adversaries with stronger computational power.
  </p>
  <p class="research-advisors">
    Advisor: Prof. Bryan Ford
  </p>
</article>

<article class="research-card">
  <div class="research-meta">Summer 2025</div>
  <h2>Proof-of-Personhood Usability Study for Digital Democracy</h2>
  <p class="research-place">
    École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)
  </p>
  <p>
    We developed a mobile-friendly web application for a proof-of-personhood
    system, with the goal of supporting usability evaluation in digital
    democracy settings.
  </p>
  <p>
    I also conducted a pilot study to test the system and gather preliminary
    feedback on the user experience.
  </p>
  <p class="research-advisors">
    Advisor: Prof. Bryan Ford
  </p>
</article>

<article class="research-card">
  <div class="research-meta">2023 – 2024</div>
  <h2>Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning</h2>
  <p class="research-place">
    Sharif University of Technology
  </p>
  <p>
    I studied privacy-preserving mechanisms for federated learning, with a
    focus on secure aggregation and multi-party computation techniques.
  </p>
  <p>
    During this work, I identified a completeness issue in an IEEE TIFS 2023
    verifiable aggregation protocol and proposed a fix to restore
    completeness.
  </p>
  <p class="research-links">
    <a href="https://ftm-zarin.github.io/portfolio/assets/files/On_compeletness_of_EVP.pdf" target="_blank">Manuscript</a>
  </p>
  <p class="research-advisors">
    Advisor: Dr. Shahram Khazaei
  </p>
</article>

</div>
